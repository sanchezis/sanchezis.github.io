Oct 5th 2018

Europe

Air Europa (Spain) to manage and market its cargo services directly from 01 November 2018 with the creation of Air Europa Cargo (Spain).

Air Partner (UK) sold an ATR72-500, serial 776, on behalf of Investec Bank (UK). The aircraft was bought by Yeti Airlines (Nepal).

Blue Air (Romania) and two Moldavian investors, purchased Air Moldova and its debt for US$70.5 million, part cash and part debt assumption. The new business plan calls for a fleet of 14 aircraft by 2021, and eventual flights to US, Canada and China, among others, as Chisinau Airport becomes into an international hub.

DAT starts fleet renewal with first ATR72-600. First of two ATR72s is leased from Aviation for 12 years.

IATA announced global passenger traffic data for August 2018 showing that RPKs climbed 6.4% yoy, with ASKs up 5.5%, and load factor up 0.7% points to 85.3%.

IATA released data for global air freight markets showing that FTKs rose 2.3% in August 2018 yoy, with AFTKs up 4.5%. This was the sixth month in a row that capacity growth outstripped demand growth. Yields, however, appear to be holding up.

Canada

First Air (Canada) appoints Chris Avery as its new President and CEO, replacing Brock Friesen, while Johnny Adams will concurrently take on a greater role in the airline as Executive Chairman. Avery will lead the carrier as part of the merged operations with Canadian North.

Makivik (Canada) and the Inuvialuit Corporate Group (Canada) announced they have signed a definitive agreement to merge First Air and Canadian North, following the receipt of government regulatory approvals, The parties expect to complete the transaction by end of 2018.

West Wind Aviation (Canada) appointed Michael Rodyniuk as its new CEO, President and Accountable Executive.

South America

Avianca Argentina to take delivery of two A320s over the next month, to cover the new Buenos Aires - Sao Paulo route, and three ATR72s to strengthen services.

Brazil's Cade to carry out additional inquiries into the proposed joint venture between Correios (Brazil) and Azul (Brazil) before making a final decision on the case.

MAPLinhas Aereas (Brazil) took delivery of one leased ATR72-200.

CIS

Yakutia (Russia) is raising serious concerns among national authorities, as the head of the region Aisen Nikolayev questioned the financial transparency of the airline.

Russia to support an additional subsidization of US$5 million for passenger traffic to the Far East.

Asia

Avation (Singapore) delivered the first new ATR72-600 aircraft to Danish Air Transport.

Hunnu Air (Mongolia) to start new ATR72 service from Ulan-Ude to Manzhouli on 11 October 2018.

IndiGo (India) to start new service from Amritsar to Dubai on 28 October, from Kochi and Ahmedabad on 01 and 02 November 2018, respectively.

Japan Air Commuter to retire one Q400, serial 4158, from end November 2018.

Nepal Airlines management was directed to formally begin the process of converting the carrier into a company.

Pakistan International Airlines has been run like a non-business entity, governed by an unprofessional Board, managed by a CEO lacking industry specific experience, says a special audit report. Recommendations include a review of bilateral agreements with Gulf and Turkish airlines, as well as a new Board and new CEO.

TransNusa (Indonesia) phased out its last operational Fokker aircraft, after selling the F50 to Leascor (Philippines). Its remaining F70 is already parked and will be sold soon.

Vietnam is considering a five-year extension to the maximum operational age of passenger and cargo aircraft - from 20 to 25 years, and from 25 to 30 years, respectively.

Africa

Africa World Airlines (Ghana) to offer ERJ145 service from Monrovia to Freetown on 01 October 2018. Overall routing will become Accra - Freetown - Monrovia.

Cape Verde discarded the idea of subsidizing Binter Cabo Verde.

Ethiopian Airlines plans to build a new airport with annual capacity of 80 million passengers in Bishoftu, about 48 km southeast of the capital Addis Ababa.

Fastjet evaluates ATR sub-leases - Management evaluating options, including the potential sub-lease of the aircraft.

Overland Airways (Nigeria) to launch new Lagos - Cotonou - Niamey service in October 2018.
